# Privacy Policy — GlowGuard

**Last updated: July 2026**

GlowGuard does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data.

## What GlowGuard accesses

- **Your settings** (dim level, blue light filter preset, automatic mode, accessibility toggles, on/off state) are stored **locally in your browser** using the browser's storage API. They never leave your device. If your browser has extension sync enabled, your browser — not GlowGuard — may sync them between your own devices.
- **The current tab's address** is read only when you open the GlowGuard popup, and only to detect browser-internal pages (such as `chrome://` or `about:` pages) where the extension cannot run, so it can show an appropriate message. This information is used momentarily and is never stored or transmitted.
- **Page content** is not read, collected, or transmitted. GlowGuard only adds visual overlay elements and reads the page's background color to decide how much to dim.

## What GlowGuard does NOT do

- No analytics or tracking of any kind
- No advertising
- No external network requests — all code and assets are bundled inside the extension
- No user accounts
- No selling or sharing of data (there is no data to sell or share)

## Permissions

- **storage** — save your preferences locally
- **alarms** — update dimming at scheduled times of day in automatic mode
- **activeTab** — detect restricted pages when the popup opens (as described above)
- **host access** — inject the dimming overlay on the pages you visit
